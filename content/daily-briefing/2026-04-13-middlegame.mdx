# Middlegame Daily
## 2026-04-13

The AI buildout is getting larger and more political at the same time. The clearest signal in today’s memo is not just that hyperscalers plan to spend [$660-690 billion on capex in 2026](https://completeaitraining.com/news/nvidia-leads-ai-infrastructure-buildout-as-hyperscalers/). It is that the physical system required to absorb that spend is starting to show its own resistance points.

### Energy

Nuclear keeps moving from narrative trade to infrastructure thesis. Bank of America now frames the category as a [$10 trillion opportunity](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/articles/nuclear-stock-face-off-oklo-175100560.html) tied directly to AI-driven power demand. That matters because the market is starting to treat reliable baseload power as a strategic input to compute expansion, not just a utility issue. The catch is execution. Oklo still sits under pressure, with shares down 40% in 2026 and real questions around regulatory timing and commercial viability, which means the market still distinguishes between the need for nuclear and the investability of specific names.

More immediate power supply is showing up through incumbent operators. NRG has expanded its data center power agreements to [445 megawatts contracted and a 5.4 gigawatt pipeline](https://ca.finance.yahoo.com/news/nrg-expands-data-center-role-111322399.html), while buying LS Power assets to expand generation capacity. That is a more practical signal than the SMR enthusiasm. It shows existing power players are already repositioning around data center load growth.

### Electrical Infrastructure

The second-order winners remain intact. Hubbell posted [mid-single-digit organic growth](https://ca.finance.yahoo.com/news/assessing-hubbell-hubb-valuation-strong-090952658.html) in Electrical Solutions with margin improvement tied to data center demand. Vertiv is up [61.8% year to date](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/articles/artificial-intelligence-ai-stock-market-163500723.html) after a 42.6% gain in 2025. The message is straightforward: when capital floods into compute, power distribution, cooling, and other enabling hardware capture the demand faster than broad AI narratives do.

This part of the trade still looks cleaner than many of the more speculative AI exposures. Power and cooling are not optional.

### Compute

The scale is getting harder to ignore. The five largest US cloud providers are planning [$660-690 billion of capex in 2026](https://completeaitraining.com/news/nvidia-leads-ai-infrastructure-buildout-as-hyperscalers/), with more than 23 gigawatts of data center capacity under construction globally. That is the core buildout signal.

But the more interesting signal sits next to it. Community resistance is starting to become operational friction. In places like Archbald, Pennsylvania, residents are pushing back on large projects over environmental concerns and electric bills, and one [18-data-center campus has already hit regulatory roadblocks](https://www.newson6.com/politics/nationwide-boom-in-ai-data-centers-stirs-resistance). That matters because the bottleneck is no longer just chips, transformers, and power generation. Local political tolerance is entering the stack.

Japan’s additional [$16 billion subsidy package for Rapidus](https://www.taipeitimes.com/News/biz/archives/2026/04/13/2003855475) belongs in the same story. States are not treating advanced chip manufacturing as a normal industrial policy issue anymore. They are treating it as strategic capacity.

### The Thread

Today’s pattern is simple: capital is still moving aggressively into the AI buildout, but the constraint layer is getting more visible. Power, electrical equipment, semiconductor capacity, and local permitting are all tightening around the same wave of demand.

That is what the Agency Era looks like in the physical world. Intelligence may be getting cheaper, but the systems needed to deploy it at scale are getting more contested, more expensive, and more politically consequential.
