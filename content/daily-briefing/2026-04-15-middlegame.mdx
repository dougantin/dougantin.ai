The market is getting clearer about what the AI buildout actually needs. Not just chips or data centers, but power, electrical equipment, and the raw materials that make both possible. The most important signal today is Oracle’s decision to scale its Bloom Energy fuel-cell procurement from [1.2 GW to 2.8 GW](https://go.theregister.com/feed/www.theregister.com/2026/04/14/oracle_bloom_fuel_cells/) under a master services agreement tied to its massive compute ambitions. When grid interconnection queues stretch toward seven years, on-site generation stops looking like a workaround and starts looking like the new default.

## The power stack is moving on its own timeline

Oracle’s move matters because it reframes the bottleneck. The constraint is no longer simply access to capital or even access to silicon; it is access to dependable electricity on a schedule that matches AI demand. That is why the market rewarded the announcement so aggressively, with [Bloom Energy up nearly 20% and Oracle higher as well](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/oracle-stock-pops-as-company-agrees-to-buy-fuel-cell-power-from-bloom-energy-144532508.html). The willingness to buy distributed generation outright suggests hyperscalers are prepared to route around utility delays rather than wait for them to resolve.

That thesis is echoed in adjacent data. The so-called green AI data center market is projected to grow from [$77.22 billion in 2025 to $111.04 billion by 2030](https://www.globenewswire.com/fr/news-release/2026/04/14/3273669/28124/en/Green-AI-Data-Center-Market-Market-Report-2026-111-Bn-Opportunities-Trends-Competitive-Landscape-Strategies-and-Forecasts-2020-2025-2025-2030F-2035F.html), and US battery storage installations reached [57 GWh in 2025, with projections for 70 GWh this year](https://www.insidermonkey.com/blog/revised-for-ghazal-10-best-energy-storage-stocks-to-buy-according-to-hedge-funds-needs-changes-1736045/). In other words: if the grid cannot expand fast enough, generation and storage will come to the load.

## Electrification is becoming the AI trade beneath the AI trade

The more durable investment theme may be electrification rather than compute in the narrow sense. That is the logic behind [Tema’s VOLT ETF crossing $500 million in assets under management](https://www.financialcontent.com/article/bizwire-2026-4-14-tema-electrification-etf-volt-surpasses-500-million-in-aum) in under 18 months. Investors are increasingly treating transformers, grid software, storage, and power equipment as first-order beneficiaries of AI rather than peripheral suppliers.

The software layer is keeping pace with the hardware layer. The market for edge AI in smart grids is projected to rise from [$15.49 billion to $19.46 billion in the near term and reach $48.91 billion by 2030](https://www.globenewswire.com/fr/news-release/2026/04/14/3273679/28124/en/Edge-AI-in-Smart-Grids-Market-Report-2026-48-91-Bn-Opportunities-Trends-Competitive-Landscape-Strategies-and-Forecasts-2020-2025-2025-2030F-2035F.html), while the broader [AI-powered smart grid market is forecast to grow to $12.79 billion by 2030](https://www.globenewswire.com/fr/news-release/2026/04/14/3273684/28124/en/AI-Powered-Smart-Grid-Market-Research-Report-2026-12-79-Bn-Opportunities-Trends-Competitive-Landscape-Strategies-and-Forecasts-2020-2025-2025-2030F-2035F.html). This is what it looks like when AI demand spills backward into the grid itself: more sensing, more automation, and more software tasked with keeping unstable systems upright.

## Compute keeps growing, but the bottleneck is physical

The data center growth projections remain large enough to matter. The multi-tenant data center market is expected to grow from [$58.86 billion in 2025 to $99.35 billion by 2030](https://www.globenewswire.com/fr/news-release/2026/04/14/3273667/28124/en/Multi-tenant-Data-Center-Market-Analysis-Report-2026-99-35-Bn-Opportunities-Trends-Competitive-Landscape-Strategies-and-Forecasts-2020-2025-2025-2030F-2035F.html), and the build-to-suit segment is projected to reach [roughly $35.94 billion by 2030](https://www.globenewswire.com/fr/news-release/2026/04/14/3273675/28124/en/Build-to-Suit-Data-Center-Market-Report-2026-35-Bn-Opportunities-Trends-Competitive-Landscape-Strategies-and-Forecasts-2020-2025-2025-2030F-2035F.html). But those numbers read differently in light of Oracle’s fuel-cell deal: the real race is not just for server halls, but for energized, usable capacity.

That is why adjacent moves like [Blackstone’s new data center REIT registration](https://therealdeal.com/national/2026/04/14/blackstone-lays-more-groundwork-for-data-center-reit/) and [Vertiv’s acquisition of BMarko Structures’ fabrication facility](http://www.brandiconimage.com/2026/04/vertiv-acquires-bmarko-structures-to.html) matter. The infrastructure buildout is becoming vertically integrated, because delay in any single layer now threatens the economics of the entire AI stack.

## Critical minerals are no longer a side story

If electricity and compute are the visible bottlenecks, materials are the slower-moving constraint underneath them. The [rare earth market is projected to expand from $3.95 billion to $6.28 billion by 2030](https://investingnews.com/rare-earth-market-enters-hypergrowth-phase-as-global-demand-surges/), while the broader [critical minerals market could reach $715.66 billion by 2035](https://www.globenewswire.com/fr/news-release/2026/04/14/3273611/0/en/Critical-Minerals-Market-Size-to-Surpass-USD-715-66-Billion-by-2035-SNS-Insider.html). Even the less glamorous wiring layer is tightening, with the [copper wire and cable market projected to grow from $182 billion in 2025 to $324.7 billion by 2032](https://www.openpr.com/news/4468779/copper-wire-cable-market-2026-2032-industry-insights-swot).

The important point is not that every market forecast will prove right. It is that AI is now large enough to drag power systems, industrial supply chains, and commodity markets into a single narrative. The trade is broadening from intelligence to infrastructure.

## Bottom line

Today’s memo reinforces a simple view: AI capex is becoming power capex. Oracle’s [2.8 GW Bloom agreement](https://go.theregister.com/feed/www.theregister.com/2026/04/14/oracle_bloom_fuel_cells/) is the cleanest proof that hyperscalers will pay up to secure electrons, not just GPUs. Once that happens, everything downstream of electrification, from grid software to copper to rare earth supply, starts looking less like a niche and more like core AI exposure.
